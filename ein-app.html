Welcome to the EIN Application Assistant

We'll help you get your Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS quickly and easily.

What is an EIN?

An Employer Identification Number (EIN) is also known as a Federal Tax Identification Number. It's used to identify your business entity for tax purposes. Think of it as a Social Security Number for your business.

Why do you need an EIN?

  • To open a business bank account
  • To file tax returns for your business
  • To hire employees
  • To establish business credit

Good news! Getting an EIN is:

  • Free - The IRS doesn't charge any fee
  • Fast - You can get it immediately if you apply online
  • Simple - We'll guide you through every step

Ready to get your EIN? We'll walk you through the process step by step.

Business Information

Let's start with some basic information about your business.

Enter the full legal name of your LLC or corporation exactly as it appears on your formation documents.

Example: "Mountain Bike Adventures, LLC" or "Sunshine Bakery, Inc."

Select the type of business entity you've formed.

If you just formed an LLC, select either "Limited Liability Company (Single-Member)" if you're the only owner, or "Limited Liability Company (Multi-Member)" if there are multiple owners.

Enter the physical address of your business. This cannot be a P.O. Box.

Responsible Party Information

The IRS requires information about the person responsible for the business.

Who is a "Responsible Party"?

A responsible party is the person who controls, manages, or directs the business and the disposition of its funds and assets. For most small businesses, this is the owner, managing member, or president.

The responsible party must have a Social Security Number (SSN) or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN).

Your SSN is required by the IRS. This information is kept secure and is used for identification purposes only.

Business Activity

Tell us about what your business does.

Provide a brief but specific description of your business activities.

Example: "Retail bakery selling bread, pastries, and cakes" or "Software development consulting services"

If you plan to hire employees within the next 12 months, select "Yes".

Note: Members of an LLC are not considered employees for this question.

Choose Your Submission Method

You can get your EIN in one of the following ways:

Online Submission

Get your EIN immediately after submission. Available Monday-Friday, 7am-10pm Eastern Time.

Fax Submission

Receive your EIN within 4 business days. Good option if online submission is unavailable.

Mail Submission

Receive your EIN within 4-5 weeks. Use if other methods aren't available to you.
